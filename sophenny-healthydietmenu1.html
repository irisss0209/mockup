3. Sautéed Steak with Broccoli and Mushroom Stir-Fry,
Served with Scrambled Eggs

Ingredients Instructions
Steak 1. Thaw the steak and pat dry with kitchen paper to remove excess blood. Season with sea salt, black pepper, and a little oil, evenly coating the steak. Marinate for 15-20 minutes. (After thawing, there is no need to rinse the steak with water.)
Broccoli 2. Blanch the broccoli in boiling water for 1-2 minutes, then remove and set aside.
Mushrooms 3. Heat a pan over high heat, add oil, and sear the steak on both sides for about 1 minute each side. When one side starts to release its juices, it's time to flip. In the final 15 seconds, add a small amount of butter to enhance the flavor.
Eggs 4. Without washing the pan, sauté garlic until fragrant, then add sliced mushrooms and stir-fry until they release their juices. Finally, add the blanched broccoli and season with sea salt and black pepper before removing from heat.
Milk 5. For the scrambled eggs, mix one egg with 20-25ml of milk, whisk until combined, then cook over low heat. Use a spatula to slowly push the egg mixture towards the center of the pan until cooked.
Garlic 6. Use a spatula to slowly push the egg mixture towards the center of the pan until cooked.

5. Steamed Egg with Shrimp, Tofu, and Broccoli

Ingredients Instructions
Eggs 1. Beat 2 eggs with salt and 1.5 cups of water until well mixed.
Tofu 2. Cut the tofu into cubes and place them in a bowl. Pour the egg mixture over the tofu.
Shrimp 3. Arrange blanched broccoli and shrimp on top of the tofu.
Broccoli 4. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap, poke small holes, and steam over boiling water for 18 minutes.

Simple Healthy Eating Tips

College students should focus on eating foods that provide ample energy and nutrition while maintaining a healthy overall lifestyle.

Vegetables and fruits

Vegetables and fruits are rich in vitamins, minerals, and fiber, which help strengthen the immune system, provide energy, and promote digestive health. Diverse choices ensure a variety of nutrients.

Whole grains

Choose whole grain foods such as whole wheat bread, brown rice, and whole grain pasta, which provide more fiber and nutrients, helping to maintain satiety and stable blood sugar levels.

High-protein foods

Protein is an essential component of body tissues, particularly important for college students, especially those who engage in sports or physical exercise. Choose high-protein foods such as chicken, fish, beans, nuts, and dairy products.

Healthy snacks

Opt for nutritious snacks like fruits, nuts, yogurt, or whole grain crackers instead of processed snacks high in sugar and unhealthy fats. These snacks can provide sustained energy and keep hunger at bay between meals.

Healthy fats

Balancing intake of healthy fats is crucial for maintaining brain health and cellular function. Opt for foods rich in monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fats such as olive oil, avocado, nuts, and fish.

Water

Maintaining adequate hydration is essential for overall health. Water is important for regulating body temperature, digestion, and metabolism. Minimize intake of sugary drinks and caffeinated beverages, and choose water as the primary beverage.
